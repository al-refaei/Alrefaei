SUCCESS STORIES

In October 2013 my cousin asked me if I want to work with him at his special center for artificial limbs as a data entry and customer service. I started working by inserting the previous patients information which were exactly 35 patients. There are many types of artificial limbs, and each one has different price. The regular one which all people prefer its cost was $1000, and this amount was too big for some people because of the Syrian Revolution. The economic situation was collapsed.

In January 2014, I remember that it was very cold day, a man who lost his tow legs came to the center asking if we can make tow artificial limbs for him for free because he cannot pay that amount of money which at least was $2500. My cousin also cannot pay that amount but he said to me take his personal information and send him home. I asked him why you have done that, he was very upset and said we have to collect as much as possible of patients' information and ask some charitable organizations to help these people. There were a lot of people who lost one of their limbs because of daily indiscriminate shelling of civilians.

So, we collected like 400 patients' information during one month. Then, we started to contact all charitable organizations to ask them to help these people, and we provided the with all the information needed. We got the materials needed to make 100 artificial limbs from one charitable organization and 300 from another one. The hard working for one month on the computer to insert all the data related to the patients' information and organize it and then create a way to visualize it was gone immediately when I saw the first patient's smile when we give him his artificial limb for free. We knew that we helped even if a small number compared to the actual number of patients but it was really great.

REFLECTION

When I first came to International Islamic University Malaysia, I thought the system of this university will be like my hometown Syria. The system in Syria was so difficult because there are carry marks and there are assignments no group projects nothing. The final exam was 100% out of 100%, which was really difficult to pass any subject. On the other hand, the system here in IIUM is easy and helpful for the students. There are assignments, group project and other activities which really provide a helpful hand to the students. These kind of activities empower so many skills for the students, like in my case, I was facing huge problems and tremendous fear when I was doing the presentation especially when the attendance was large, in addition I needed a lot of time to prepare for the presentation. However, this problem persisted for two semesters but then I really gain the ability to prepare for the presentations demand a short time. I also got some experiences of the presentation skills as well.

After four years living inside the campus, I have developed many relationship from many countries. I learned a lot about different countries and cultures from around the world. As if you traveled around the world. Living inside the campus provides me with many soft skill. Before I moved here I was a little bit shy to talk to people and make friends, but now I am ready to talk to anyone even if it is the first time I meet him. Not only that, I joined some activities inside and outside the university, I went to trips with many students and I got to know them and make friendship with them until this moment.

Until the last semester, I was not think about the fact that how the real companies work and what are the procedures needed to be known for the real working environment. In this semester, I am taking English For Occupational Purposes subject. With the help of this subject, I got to understand how to write official letters, MEMO report, proposal report and so on. This subject help me to gain the knowledge and the imagination if how to be in a company before I joined one. I had a good experience on how to deal with company members, and what I needed to be a successful company member. All thanks and best regards to madam Faridah Abdul Malik for her efforts to teach us all those needed skills to be well-prepared to join a company.
